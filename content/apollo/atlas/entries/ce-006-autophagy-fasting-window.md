## The Stance

Stefan's stance: intermittent fasting is a tool, not an identity. The 16:8 window is the conservative target. Members who add fasting on top of a sustainable training and protein protocol see a benefit. Members who use fasting to mask under-eating see the opposite.

## Plain English

The body runs maintenance protocols when food is absent for long enough. Around 12 hours into a fast, the metabolic switch starts to engage. By 14 to 16 hours, autophagy (the body's cellular cleanup) is measurably upregulated. The protocol is a daily eating window of 8 to 10 hours, which lands the fasting window around 14 to 16 hours including overnight sleep.

The protocol does not work if it cuts protein intake below the leucine threshold (see ce-013). Members on Apollo's IF protocol still hit three meals worth of protein inside the eating window.

## Technical

Fasting reduces nutrient signalling through mTORC1 and raises AMPK activity, which together upregulate autophagy in fasted tissues. The metabolic-switch literature places the meaningful threshold for human autophagy upregulation in the 12 to 18 hour fasting range, although direct measurement in humans is methodologically harder than the rodent literature suggests. Improved insulin sensitivity, reduced inflammation markers, and modest fat loss are observed in controlled-feeding studies.

The evidence-level here is honest: rodent autophagy data is strong; human translation is medium; effect sizes vary across populations.

## The Studies

In Cell (2019), Levine and Kroemer reviewed the biological functions of autophagy genes and the disease-prevention implications, providing the foundation for human autophagy claims.

In Obesity (2018), Anton and colleagues reviewed the metabolic-switch literature and proposed that the fasting threshold relevant to human health benefits sits at 12 to 18 hours, which is the basis for the 16:8 protocol Apollo runs.

## Doctrine bridge

This is the protocol that demands the doctrine *effortless, not extreme* most strictly. Members who run extended fasts (24 to 72 hours) without supervision regularly under-eat protein and lose lean mass. The 16:8 window is the dose. Deeper fasts are someone else's protocol.
