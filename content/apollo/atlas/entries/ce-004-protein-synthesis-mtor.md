## The Stance

Stefan's stance: stop counting and start anchoring. The plate-method protocol works because it puts a leucine-sufficient dose at every sitting, without a tracking app and without a scale.

## Plain English

Muscle is built when each meal contains enough high-quality protein to trigger an anabolic response. The body does not pool protein across the day and dose itself evenly. It runs a check at each meal. If the dose is below threshold, the response is muted, regardless of how much protein you ate at breakfast.

For most adult men, that means around 30 to 50 grams of protein per meal, three to four times daily. Palm-sized portions of meat, fish, eggs, dairy, or a whey serving plus food. Apollo runs this without macros and without weighing.

## Technical

The mTORC1 (mechanistic target of rapamycin complex 1) pathway is the central regulator of muscle protein synthesis. Activation requires reaching a leucine threshold of approximately 2.5 to 3 grams per meal, which corresponds to roughly 0.4 to 0.5 grams of high-quality protein per kilogram of body weight per sitting. Three to four meals at this dose maximise the daily MPS area-under-the-curve, with diminishing returns above approximately 1.6 grams per kilogram per day.

## The Studies

In British Journal of Sports Medicine (2018), Morton and colleagues conducted the meta-analysis that established roughly 1.6 grams per kilogram per day as the ceiling above which additional protein produces minimal hypertrophy benefit. The Apollo target sits inside this evidence range.

In the Journal of the International Society of Sports Nutrition (2018), Schoenfeld and Aragon reviewed the evidence on per-meal protein distribution and recommended three to four feedings of 0.4 to 0.55 grams per kilogram each, which is the per-meal target Apollo carries.
