## The Stance

Stefan's stance: most men under-sleep because their bedroom is too warm and their evening too still. The fix is not a supplement. The fix is the thermostat and a shower.

## Plain English

The body falls asleep on a temperature descent. Sleep onset typically requires a core body temperature drop of 1 to 2°C. The room temperature, the bedding, and the pre-bed heating all influence how steeply the descent runs. Bedroom around 16 to 19°C. A hot shower or bath 60 to 90 minutes before bed paradoxically accelerates the drop, because it pulls heat to the skin which then radiates out.

This is the input most men never adjust because they have always slept warm. Try the cool room for two weeks and the difference is measurable.

## Technical

Sleep onset is gated by a thermoregulatory cascade involving distal vasodilation (hands and feet warming), which dumps core heat into the periphery and lowers core body temperature by 1 to 2°C. Pre-bed warming via warm bath or shower exaggerates this effect via subsequent vasodilation at lights-out. Cool ambient temperature (16 to 19°C) supports core cooling throughout the night, particularly during slow-wave sleep when thermoregulation is suppressed.

## The Studies

In Sleep Medicine Reviews (2008), Lack and colleagues reviewed the relationship between body temperature and sleep, establishing the thermoregulatory descent as a primary gating mechanism for sleep onset.

In Sleep Medicine Reviews (2019), Haghayegh and colleagues conducted a meta-analysis on pre-bed warm-water bathing, demonstrating reductions in sleep latency of around 8 to 10 minutes when the warm shower or bath was timed 1 to 2 hours before bed.
