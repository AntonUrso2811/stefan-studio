## The Stance

Stefan's stance: grip is not vanity. Grip is decade-scale capacity. Apollo trains it weekly because the literature is unambiguous and the protocol is simple.

## Plain English

Grip strength is an unusually strong predictor of how long and how well a person lives. The PURE study tracked 140,000 adults across 17 countries. Each 5kg decrease in grip strength was associated with a 16% increase in all-cause mortality. Grip is also one of the easiest things to train. Carry heavy things and hang from bars.

This is the kind of input that does not look like fitness in the next 30 days. It looks like fitness in the next 30 years.

## Technical

Grip strength is hypothesised to function as a proxy for total muscle quality, total body strength, and neural drive. The mortality association is robust across populations and survives adjustment for age, sex, education, alcohol, smoking, and physical activity. Causal mechanisms are debated, but the marker reliably tracks broader functional capacity. Trainable via loaded carries, hangs, thick-grip work, and forearm-specific accessory work.

## The Studies

In The Lancet (2015), Leong and colleagues conducted the PURE study (140,000 adults, 17 countries, 4-year follow-up) and demonstrated that grip strength was a stronger predictor of all-cause and cardiovascular mortality than systolic blood pressure.

In Clinical Interventions in Aging (2019), Bohannon reviewed grip strength as a biomarker for older adults, summarising the cross-population evidence and the case for routine measurement and training.
