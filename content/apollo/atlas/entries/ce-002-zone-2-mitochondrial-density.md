## The Stance

Stefan's stance: Zone 2 is the input most men skip because it does not feel like training. That is precisely why it is non-negotiable. The aerobic base is the floor of every other capacity in the system.

## Plain English

Zone 2 is steady cardio at the pace just below the point your legs would burn. You can hold a conversation. You would rather not, but you can. That is the dose. Done at 180 minutes per week across three to four sessions, it builds the engine the rest of the system runs on.

This is the input that pays in years rather than months. A man who runs Zone 2 consistently from age 40 has a different VO₂max trajectory at 60 than a man who chases harder intervals and skips the base.

## Technical

Zone 2 sits below the first lactate threshold, where lactate production and clearance are still in balance. Training at this intensity drives mitochondrial biogenesis primarily through PGC-1α signalling, expands the capillary network feeding type I fibres, and improves fat-oxidation capacity at sub-threshold intensities. The downstream effect is a higher VO₂max ceiling over months and a lower resting heart rate within weeks.

## The Studies

In Sports Medicine (2018), San Millán and Brooks established the metabolic-flexibility framework that distinguishes Zone 2 training from higher-intensity work, with measurable differences in lactate clearance and substrate use.

In Comprehensive Physiology (2015), Hellsten and Nyberg reviewed the cardiovascular adaptations to exercise training, anchoring the claim that aerobic base work produces structural changes (capillary density, mitochondrial density) that intensity work alone does not.
