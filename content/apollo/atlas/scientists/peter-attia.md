## Plain English

A longevity physician who reframes fitness as decade-scale capacity rather than near-term aesthetics. His phrase *"the four horsemen"* (atherosclerotic disease, cancer, neurodegeneration, type 2 diabetes plus its metabolic precursors) is the clearest articulation of why the protocols Apollo runs matter past the next 90 days.

## What Apollo uses

Apollo's Phase 4 is essentially the operational layer of Attia's longevity frame. Specifically:

- **Zone 2 cardio as mitochondrial-density work.** Apollo's training system in M04 and M08 carries this verbatim. The dose, the rationale, the pacing rule that "you can hold a conversation but you would rather not" are all Attia's framing.
- **VO₂max as a mortality predictor, not a vanity number.** M19 leans on Attia's reading of Mandsager 2018 and the Cleveland Clinic data. The Atlas carries the citation.
- **Grip strength as a longevity marker.** Apollo's training system tracks farmer carries and dead hangs as standard work. The justification chain runs through Attia's writing on the topic.
- **Protein anchor at 1.6 to 2.2 grams per kilogram per day.** Apollo's nutrition framework in M05 and M10 is calibrated to this range. Attia has been the loudest popular voice making the case.

## What Apollo does not echo

- Apollo does not use bloodwork as a product. Attia's practice runs on dense lab panels. Apollo does not require, sell, or interpret labs. The line is deliberate. M19 references markers, but the protocol does not depend on them.
- Apollo does not pursue pharmaceutical optimisation. Attia's clinical practice includes off-label and longevity-pharma work that sits outside Apollo's scope by design.
- Apollo does not chase the maximum dose. The Phase 4 longevity frame is *capacity-preserving*, not *capacity-maximising*. Where Attia advocates the upper range, Apollo runs the sustainable middle.
