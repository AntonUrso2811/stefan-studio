## Frameworks / protocols

**The Protocol Hierarchy — three tiers**

**Tier 1: Non-Negotiables** (must implement — without these, nothing else matters)
1. Training consistency — follow Phase 2 program; progressive overload on primary lifts
2. Protein target — hit daily goal, every day
3. Sleep — 7–9 hours, consistent times
4. Caloric alignment — match your goal (deficit/maintenance/surplus)

**Tier 2: High-Impact Additions** (after Tier 1 is solid)
1. Meal timing around training — pre and post-workout nutrition
2. Recovery tracking — HRV/RHR, subjective assessment, training adjustment
3. Zone 2 conditioning — 2–3 sessions/week
4. Stress management — daily breathing, nervous system regulation
5. Intermittent fasting — if appropriate for goals/lifestyle

**Tier 3: Optimisation** (when Tier 1 and 2 are automatic)
1. Carb cycling
2. Advanced supplementation (Tier 2/3 supplements)
3. Sauna and cold protocols
4. Nutrient timing precision
5. Intensity techniques (rest-pause, drop sets, tempo)

**The 6-Question Assessment** — primary goal · body fat estimate · training age · schedule (days/session length/meal prep time) · current weak points · what's worked before

**The 8-Week Structure**
- Weeks 1–2: Foundation Reset + Habit Lock-In (re-establish baselines, adherence focus)
- Weeks 3–4: Push Phase + Deload (first intensity block, then recovery week)
- Weeks 5–6: Intensification Begins + Intensity Push (second block, harder)
- Weeks 7–8: Assessment Push + Deload (final push, phase review)

**The Weekly Review** — Sunday assessment of training, nutrition, recovery, mindset markers. Adjust next week based on trends.

**The Traffic Light System (Phase 2 variant)** — progress on track (green) · adjust one variable (amber) · stop and reassess (red).

The Protocol Hierarchy - PHASE 2 INTEGRATION
You cannot do everything at once. Trying to implement every strategy from every module
simultaneously leads to overwhelm and abandonment.

> The key is prioritization - selecting the highest-impact elements for your specific situation and
building a sustainable system.

The Protocol Hierarchy

Not all elements are equal. Some are foundational and non-negotiable. Others are optimizations that
matter only after the foundation is solid.

Tier 1: Non-Negotiables (Must implement)

These are the pillars. Without these, nothing else matters.

 1. Training consistency - Follow the Phase 2 program. Show up for every scheduled session.
   Progressive overload on primary lifts.

 2. Protein target - Hit your daily protein goal. Every single day.

 3. Sleep - 7-9 hours nightly. Consistent sleep and wake times. Non-negotiable.

 4. Caloric alignment - Calories match your goal (deficit for cutting, maintenance for recomp,
   surplus for gaining).

> If you only do these four things consistently, you will make significant progress. Everything else is
optimization.

Tier 2: High-Impact Additions (Implement after Tier 1 is solid)

These meaningfully enhance results once the foundation is in place.

 1. Meal timing around training - Pre and post-workout nutrition optimized.

 2. Recovery tracking - HRV or RHR monitoring, subjective assessment, adjusting training based
   on recovery status.

 3. Zone 2 conditioning - 2-3 sessions per week for cardiovascular health and recovery.

 4. Stress management practice - Daily breathing practice, nervous system regulation.

 5. Intermittent fasting - If appropriate for your goals and lifestyle.

Tier 3: Optimization (Implement when Tier 1 and 2 are automatic)

These provide marginal gains. Only pursue when everything else is dialled in.

 1. Carb cycling - Varying carbs based on training days.

 2. Advanced supplementation - Tier 2 and 3 supplements beyond the basics.

 3. Sauna and cold protocols - Strategic heat and cold exposure.

 4. Nutrient timing precision - Specific timing of carbs, protein pulsing, etc.

 5. Intensity techniques - Rest-pause, drop sets, tempo manipulation.

Building Your Protocol: The Assessment - PHASE 2
Before building your protocol, honestly assess your current status.

Question 1: What is your primary goal for Phase 2?

  • Fat loss (get leaner while maintaining muscle)

  • Recomposition (build muscle while losing fat)

  • Muscle gain (add size with minimal fat)

This determines your caloric approach and training emphasis.

Question 2: What is your current body fat percentage (estimate)?

  • Over 20%: Prioritize fat loss or aggressive recomp

  • 15-20%: Recomp or moderate cut viable

  • Under 15%: Lean gaining or recomp viable

Question 3: How long have you been training consistently?

  • Under 1 year: Recomp highly effective; focus on progressive overload

  • 1-3 years: Dedicated phases may be more effective than recomp

  • Over 3 years: Likely need dedicated surplus for muscle gain

Question 4: What is your schedule like?

  • Training days available per week: 3 / 4 / 5

  • Time per session: 45min / 60min / 75min+

  • Meal prep time available: Minimal / Moderate / Flexible

Question 5: What are your current weak points?

  • Training: Consistency / Intensity / Technique / Weak muscle groups

  • Nutrition: Protein intake / Overall calories / Food quality / Meal timing

  • Recovery: Sleep / Stress management / Rest days / Active recovery

Question 6: What has worked for you before?

  • Training style that you enjoy and stick with

  • Eating pattern that feels sustainable

  • Recovery practices that make a difference

Use these answers to build your protocol.

Phase 2 Week-by-Week Breakdown

WEEK 1: Foundation Reset

Focus: Establish baseline, implement core habits

Training:

  • Begin Phase 2 program (Mesocycle 1 - Accumulation)

  • Focus on learning any new exercises

  • Record baseline weights for all movements

  • RPE 7-8 on working sets (do not max out week 1)

Nutrition:

  • Implement your caloric and macro targets

  • Set up tracking if using (first few days track everything)

  • Establish meal timing pattern

  • If starting IF, begin with 14:10 window (easier transition)

Recovery:

  • Lock in sleep schedule (same bed/wake time daily)

  • Begin morning recovery check (HRV/RHR or subjective rating)

  • Implement one stress management practice (choose your breathing protocol)

Tracking:

  • Daily weigh-in, record all

  • Measure waist on Day 1

  • Take progress photos on Day 1

  • Set up training log

> Week 1 Checkpoint: Protocol established. Baseline data collected. Core habits initiated.

WEEK 2: Habit Lock-In - PHASE 2 INTEGRATION
Focus: Solidify routines, push training

Training:

  • Push for progression on all lifts (add reps within range)

  • Implement weak point exercises

  • Full effort on working sets (RPE 7-8)

  • Add conditioning if not yet implemented

Nutrition:

  • Fine-tune meal timing

  • Assess hunger and energy - adjust meal sizes if needed

  • If using IF, tighten to 16:8 window

  • Identify any food prep challenges and solve them

Recovery:

  • Sleep schedule should feel natural now

  • Add evening wind-down routine if not yet implemented

  • Continue daily recovery tracking

Tracking:

  • Calculate Week 1 weight average, compare to Week 2

  • Note any patterns in recovery data

> Week 2 Checkpoint: Habits becoming automatic. Training intensity appropriate. Nutrition dialled.

WEEK 3: Push Phase - PHASE 2 INTEGRATION
Focus: Training intensity, fine-tuning

Training:

  • Push harder - seek progression on primary lifts

  • RPE 8 on most working sets

  • Focus on weak point exercises

  • Note which exercises are progressing and which are stuck

Nutrition:

  • By now you should know if calories are appropriate

  • Gaining too fast? Reduce 100-200 calories

  • Not losing? Assess tracking accuracy, then consider reduction

  • Energy good? Continue as planned

Recovery:

  • Assess accumulated fatigue - do you need more recovery?

  • Implement sauna or cold if part of your protocol

  • Continue all established practices

Tracking:

  • 3-week weight trend should show direction

  • Waist measurement comparison to Week 1

> Week 3 Checkpoint: Progression happening. Protocol adjustments made if needed. Fatigue
managed.

WEEK 4: Deload Week - PHASE 2 INTEGRATION
Focus: Active recovery, assessment

Training:

  • Reduce volume by 40-50% (fewer sets, not fewer exercises)

  • Maintain intensity (similar weights, fewer sets)

  • RPE 6-7 - stop well short of failure

  • No intensity techniques

  • Conditioning: Zone 2 only

Nutrition:

  • Maintain current calories (or slight increase if cutting)

  • Keep protein high

  • Do not use deload as excuse to eat poorly

Recovery:

  • Prioritize sleep even more than usual

  • Extra stress management practices

  • Sauna/cold protocols continue if using

  • This is active recovery, not complete rest

Tracking:

  • Monthly weigh-in average calculated

  • Full body measurements

  • Progress photos

  • Review all data

Week 4 Assessment Questions:

 1. How much weight have I lost/gained over 4 weeks?

 2. Has my waist measurement changed?

 3. Are my lifts progressing?

 4. How is my energy and recovery?

 5. What is working? What needs adjustment?

> Week 4 Checkpoint: Recovered and ready. First mesocycle complete. Data reviewed. Adjustments

  • If gaining and gaining too fast, reduce surplus

Nutrition:

  • Continue established approach

  • If energy is suffering from intensity, ensure carbs around training

  • Maintain protein regardless of everything else

  • Higher intensity means higher recovery demand

  • 7-week data should clearly show if approach is working

  • Watch for overreaching signs (elevated RHR, suppressed HRV, poor sleep, low motivation)

When to Adjust vs When to Stay the Course - PHASE 2
Most people adjust too often. They see one bad weigh-in and panic. They have one hard week and
change everything. This prevents progress.

Stay the course when:

  • You have been consistent for less than 3 weeks

  • Data shows normal fluctuation (weight varies within 1-2%)

  • One bad day or week but overall trend is correct

  • Life temporarily disrupted but returning to normal

  • You feel impatient but data shows progress

Adjust when:

  • 3+ weeks of no progress despite consistent execution

  • Clear trend in wrong direction (weight up when cutting, down when gaining)

  • Performance consistently declining

  • Energy and recovery consistently poor

  • Signs of overtraining or excessive stress

How to Adjust

When adjustment is needed, change one variable at a time. Otherwise, you cannot identify what
worked.

If fat loss has stalled (cutting):

 1. First, verify tracking accuracy (common issue)

 2. Then, reduce calories by 100-150

 3. Or add one cardio session

 4. Do not do both simultaneously

If gaining too fast (bulking):

 1. Reduce calories by 100-200

 2. Or add one cardio session

 3. Assess after 2 weeks

If not gaining (bulking):

 1. Verify calorie tracking

 2. Increase calories by 150-200

 3. Reduce cardio if excessive

 4. Assess after 2 weeks

If strength is declining:

 1. Assess recovery (sleep, stress, HRV)

 2. Assess calories (are you undereating?)

 3. Consider deload if accumulated fatigue

 4. Reduce volume before reducing intensity

If energy is consistently low:

 1. Check sleep quantity and quality

 2. Check calorie intake (deficit too aggressive?)

 3. Check carbohydrate intake (too low for activity?)

 4. Check life stress (stress bucket overflowing?)

 5. Check overtraining signs

The Traffic Light System - PHASE 2 INTEGRATION
A simple framework for daily decisions:

GREEN: Everything is going well

  • Recovery markers good

  • Energy high

  • Sleep adequate

  • Life stress manageable → Train as planned, push hard

YELLOW: Some warning signs

  • Recovery markers slightly off

  • Energy moderate

  • Sleep disrupted recently

  • Life stress elevated → Train but reduce intensity slightly, prioritize recovery

RED: Clear warning signs

  • Recovery markers suppressed

  • Energy low

  • Sleep poor for multiple days

  • Life stress high

  • Getting sick or feeling run down → Light training only or rest, full recovery focus

> Check in with yourself daily. What colour is today? Adjust accordingly.

Avoiding Common Mistakes - PHASE 2 INTEGRATION
Mistake 1: Changing too often

  • Problem: Adjusting weekly based on daily fluctuations

  • Solution: Make decisions based on 2-3 week trends, not daily data

Mistake 2: Ignoring data because you "feel fine"

  • Problem: Subjective feel can lag behind objective markers

  • Solution: Trust objective data, especially HRV/RHR suppression

Mistake 3: Not adjusting when clearly needed

  • Problem: Stubbornly following the plan when it is not working

  • Solution: After 3+ weeks of no progress with consistent execution, change something

Mistake 4: Blaming the program instead of execution

  • Problem: Thinking the program does not work when adherence is poor

  • Solution: Honestly assess adherence first; only change program if execution was good

Mistake 5: Comparing to others

  • Problem: Expecting your progress to match someone else's

  • Solution: Compare only to your own past data; everyone responds differently
