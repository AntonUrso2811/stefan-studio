## The Stance

Stefan's stance: there are five lifts that matter, and the rest is decoration. Squat, hinge, press, pull, carry. Run them with progressive overload across decades and the rest of the system has something to recover into.

## Plain English

Hypertrophy is driven by mechanical tension and a sufficient weekly dose. Apollo's training is built on five compound patterns and a volume target high enough to drive adaptation, low enough to recover from. Members do not need a different programme every block. They need the same five movements with five more kilos every quarter.

The right effort is the last two reps of every working set looking ugly. Not failure on every set. Hard, repeatable.

## Technical

Hypertrophy follows a dose-response on weekly volume up to roughly 10 to 20 hard sets per muscle group, with diminishing returns past that range for most members. Mechanical tension on type II fibres at 70 to 85% of 1RM is the dominant driver, with metabolic stress contributing additional signal at moderate-load high-rep work. mTORC1 activation post-session is sustained for roughly 24 to 48 hours, which is why sessions per muscle per week (frequency) matters as much as total volume.

## The Studies

In Journal of Sports Sciences (2017), Schoenfeld, Ogborn, and Krieger conducted the meta-analysis establishing the dose-response relationship between weekly resistance training volume and hypertrophy, with the inflection point near 10 weekly sets per muscle.

In Medicine and Science in Sports and Exercise (2019), Schoenfeld and colleagues confirmed that higher volume drives more hypertrophy in trained men, although strength gains plateau earlier than mass gains do.
