## The Stance

Stefan's stance: VO₂max is the longevity number. The Mandsager study put it on the map. The protocol is Zone 2 most of the time, intervals once a week, run for years.

## Plain English

VO₂max is the maximum amount of oxygen your body can use during peak exertion. It correlates with mortality more strongly than smoking, hypertension, or diabetes do. The Mandsager 2018 study, run on 122,000 adults at the Cleveland Clinic, showed mortality risk dropped continuously as VO₂max rose, with no upper plateau.

The protocol is straightforward. Zone 2 builds the base. One intense session per week (4x4 intervals) raises the ceiling. Run for years.

## Technical

VO₂max is measured in millilitres of oxygen per kilogram body weight per minute (mL/kg/min) at peak workload. Determined by cardiac stroke volume, oxygen-carrying capacity of blood, and peripheral oxygen extraction. Trainable in untrained adults by 15 to 25% over 8 to 12 weeks of structured training. The Mandsager dataset shows mortality risk reductions are exposure-graded across all fitness categories, including the highest decile compared to the next-highest decile.

The 4x4 protocol (Norwegian model): four minutes at 90% maximum heart rate, three minutes of active recovery, repeated four times. Once weekly is sufficient for ceiling effect.

## The Studies

In JAMA Network Open (2018), Mandsager and colleagues examined cardiorespiratory fitness and long-term mortality in 122,000 patients undergoing exercise treadmill testing at the Cleveland Clinic. The hazard ratio for the lowest fitness category compared to elite was approximately 5x higher all-cause mortality. The finding has reshaped the longevity-medicine framing.

In Journal of the American College of Cardiology (2019), Imboden and colleagues confirmed the cardiorespiratory fitness-mortality association in a healthy population, reinforcing the case that VO₂max is a primary lever rather than a secondary marker.
