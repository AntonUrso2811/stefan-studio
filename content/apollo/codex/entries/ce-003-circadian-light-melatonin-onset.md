## The Stance

Stefan's stance: morning sunlight is the cheapest and most powerful sleep protocol on the entire stack. It is also the one most men skip because it is free and feels like nothing.

## Plain English

The body decides when to release melatonin in the evening based on what it saw at sunrise. If the eyes do not see daylight in the first hour of being awake, the evening release runs late and the next night's sleep onset drifts. The fix is direct outdoor light, ideally within 30 to 60 minutes of waking, for 10 to 30 minutes.

Cloud cover is fine. Through a window does not count. Sunglasses defeat the purpose. The dose is the photons hitting the retina.

## Technical

Light exposure at sunrise stimulates melanopsin-expressing intrinsically photosensitive retinal ganglion cells, which signal to the suprachiasmatic nucleus and entrain the circadian pacemaker. Repeated daily exposure phase-locks the pacemaker against the local solar day. Downstream effects include a sharper cortisol awakening response, a forward shift in dim-light melatonin onset, and a measurable improvement in sleep latency on consistent users.

Outdoor light intensity (around 10,000 to 100,000 lux) is one to two orders of magnitude above any indoor environment, including most window-side desks.

## The Studies

In Current Biology (2013), Wright and colleagues demonstrated that natural light-dark exposure entrains the human circadian clock more rapidly than artificial light, with measurable shifts in melatonin onset within a week of camping in natural conditions.

In Science (1999), Czeisler and colleagues established the precision and stability of the human circadian pacemaker and the role of light as the dominant zeitgeber, providing the foundation for every subsequent circadian-light protocol.
