## The Stance

Stefan's stance: stop counting grams across the day. Anchor each meal with a leucine-sufficient protein source and the rest of the system handles itself.

## Plain English

Leucine is the amino acid the body uses as the main switch for muscle protein synthesis. Hit roughly 2.5 to 3 grams of leucine in a meal and the body responds. That is approximately 30 to 50 grams of meat, fish, eggs, dairy, or whey for most adult men. Below that threshold, the meal is calories without much muscle-building signal.

Run this at every meal, three to four meals daily, and the daily target takes care of itself. No tracking app required.

## Technical

Leucine is the dominant trigger for mTORC1 activation among the branched-chain amino acids. Studies of whey protein dose-response show MPS plateaus around 20 to 40 grams of high-quality protein per meal in younger men and 35 to 40 grams in older men. The leucine threshold, roughly 2.5 to 3 grams, sits inside this protein range for most leucine-dense sources. Plant-protein sources require larger absolute doses or composition strategy to hit the threshold.

## The Studies

In Sports Medicine (2014), Phillips reviewed the critical processes in exercise-induced hypertrophy, anchoring leucine threshold and dose-response framing.

In American Journal of Clinical Nutrition (2014), Witard and colleagues established the per-meal whey protein dose-response curve at rest and after resistance exercise, with MPS plateaus near 20 grams whey at rest and 40 grams post-exercise.
