## The Doctrine

> "You have spent sixty days in the forge."
> *M14 Phase 3 Overview, module lede*

The Apollo system runs on four named phases. Foundation. Forging. Ascension. Apollo. Phase 3 earns the forge metaphor. Sixty days is the threshold beyond which the work has changed the man, not just his routine.

## Why this doctrine matters for M14

The forge metaphor is load-bearing. It frames the prior sixty days as a deliberate crucible, not a trial run. The body has been heated and shaped. The framework has been pressed in. Phase 3 is the cooling. Refinement, specialisation, the move from raw stock to edged tool.

Without this doctrine, members at Day 61 act as if they are still on Day 1, just with bigger numbers. With this doctrine, members enter Phase 3 acknowledging that something durable has happened, and the work changes shape accordingly.

## The Default

When this doctrine is internalised, Phase 3 is run differently from Phase 1. The default is precision, not accumulation. The default is specialisation, not breadth. The default is the slower, more deliberate iteration of an already-functioning system.

The Phase 3 modules (M14 through M18) are designed for a member who has already changed. M15 is *advanced physical optimisation*, not *more training*. M18 is *circadian mastery*, not *try harder to sleep*.

## The Drift Check

> "If a man on Day 1 of Apollo asked me what I have learned, could I name three things I now know in my body that I did not know at Day 1?"

If the answer is no, the forge work did not land. The fix is not to push forward into Phase 3. It is to run a Phase 1 audit and rebuild any inputs that drifted.

## Cadence

**Phase-end review.** This doctrine activates only at the Phase 2 to Phase 3 threshold. Once a quarter, not weekly.

## Voice notes

The forge metaphor is Apollo-coherent (fire, craft, shaping) without crossing into Pagan-God-Mode or warrior register. It is workshop, not war. Use *crucible*, *shaping*, *temper*, *edge*, *refine*. Avoid *crush*, *forge ahead*, *grind*, *hammer through*.

The doctrine carries weight only if the prior sixty days were real. Do not invoke it for members who skipped Phase 1 or 2.
